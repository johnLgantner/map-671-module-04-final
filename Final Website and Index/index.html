Peacekeeping and Civilian Violence in the Congo

Authored by: John Gantner

The maps provided on this website look to help the viewer understand not only the dynamics of civilian violence in the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC), but international responses to it--Specifically those of the United Nations (UN). The UN has sent what it deems as peacekeeping deployments to various conflicts throughout the world since its inception in the 1940s. However, after the Cold War ended, these missions skyrocketed. Like much of the current scholarship regarding UN Peacekeeping, this site aims to demonstrate whether Peacekeepers "flock" to areas with intense violence.

The map provide simply provides locations of civilian violence in the DRC--where is there lots of violence aimed at civilians--since 1995. This data was used to create a kernel density heatmap, and then overlays the locations of all UN Peackeeping Deployments from the mission (MINUSCO) since 1998. In this map, it is seen that there is some significant correlating evidence suggesting that deployments do follow violence against civilians. This, at face value, shows that these missions (at least MINUSCO) is following its mandated activities to protect said civilians.

UN Peacekeeping Deployments Overlayed on Civilian Death

Map details

This map was made using QGIS and a number of data sources. Both the Georeferenced Peacekeeping Operation Data and the Civilian Violence Data come from the Uppsala Conflict Data Program. These data provide georeferenced points for PKO deployments and civlian death since 1995. All data, as shown in the maps, follows the covention of EPSG: 4326 and a Scale of 1:9500000.
